Local Food

Sate Ponorogo

  • Sate (grilled marinated chicken) Ponorogo different from Sate Madura, the meat sliced long thin like a fillet. The size relatively bigger with elongated slices, so that one skewer usually only contains one or two pieces of meat.
  • Sate Ponorogo through the process of soaking the spices (dibacem) so the spices soak into the meat. It served in peanut sauce, garnished with shredded shallots, chilli paste and lime juice.





Dawet Jabung

  • Typical drink from Ponorogo. Named Dawet Jabung because the origin of this dawet is Jabung Village, one of the villages in Mlarak District.
  • Dawet Jabung similar to cendol ice, but the cendol made from palm flour and without coloring. The dawet sauce is coconut milk added with palm sugar and a little salt. Usually added with tape ketan and jackfruit slices.





Jenang Mirah

  • Ponorogo typical snacks. It was named Jenang Mirah because the person who made this jenang was Ibu Mirah. It comes from Josari Village.
  • Jenang Mirah is made from ketan, coconut sugar and coconut milk. Jenang Mirah is a wet food because it only lasts one week, unless it is put in the refrigerator. It is very easy to find in a typical gift shop in Ponorogo.
